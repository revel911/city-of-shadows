## The Setting Hook: The Overwrite

Richmond has always rewritten itself — fires, war, Reconstruction, urban renewal. But something deeper is happening now. Entire narratives are being subtly altered. Not erased. Adjusted. Dates shift. Names change. Outcomes blur. And only certain people — overwhelmingly supernatural, but not always — notice the inconsistencies.

The city sits on overlapping versions of itself. Sometimes they bleed together. What is buried beneath Shockoe Bottom is not just history. It is an earlier draft of the city, still running, still changing.

The MC should let this surface slowly. Discrepancies in the Public Events Log. NPCs who contradict each other on facts about the past. A location that appears different in two players' accounts of visiting it. The Overwrite is not a villain — it is a condition.

## The Hubs

*A directory pointing at Hub docs. Deep content lives in `hubs/[name].md`.*

- **Shockoe Bottom** (Mortalis-dominant) — Richmond's oldest and most fractured district. Slave trade history, buried infrastructure, sealed chambers, and a neighborhood in constant tension between gentrification and memory. The mundane world presses hardest here, which is exactly why the supernatural runs deepest. Most active Overwrite site.

- **The Fan / Museum District** (Wild-dominant) — Victorian row houses, galleries, art schools, independent venues. The city's creative lung. Fae bleed through here. Artists make things they don't remember making. Monument Avenue scars still showing.

- **Downtown / The Canal Walk** (Night-dominant) — Finance, law firms, converted warehouses. The canal system is old and the tunnels below older still. Vampires own half the real estate through shells. The surface is glass and granite; underneath it runs with old water and older blood.

- **The University** (Power-dominant) — Virginia Commonwealth University and the Medical College of Virginia form the beating academic heart of midtown. Mages, scholars, the Silver Ladder's institutional ambitions, and at least one Consilium that pretends to be a faculty committee.

- **Creighton Court / East End** (Mortalis-dominant) — Richmond's East End public housing communities undergoing controversial "Renaissance" redevelopment. Four of the city's six major housing complexes sit within a single square mile here. Temporal displacement: different eras of the East End coexist simultaneously. Families displaced by demolition still appear in buildings that no longer exist. The Overwrite hits hardest in communities already being erased.

- **Oregon Hill** (Mortalis-dominant) — Historic working-class neighborhood overlooking the James River, built by Tredegar Iron Works employees in the 1840s. Wedged between Hollywood Cemetery, VCU campus, and the Downtown Expressway. Industrial echo: phantom factory shifts, spectral workers continuing labor that ended 150 years ago. The neighborhood resists change with the same stubbornness it always has.

### Mortalis
- **Status:** pressured
- **Key NPCs:**
  - Detective Sgt. Paulette Okafor — Mortalis, RPD cold case unit — wants the city's secrets to stay dead — Status 2 (knows too much to be fully mortal, not enough to be safe)
  - Father Benedikt Hruska — Mortalis, St. Peter's Catholic on Grace St — wants to hold the neighborhood together as it changes — Status 1
  - Emmett Daye — Mortalis, Hollywood Cemetery groundskeeper (40 years) — wants to make sense of what he's seen — Status 0
- **Current agenda:** Keep the mundane world from noticing the Overwrite. Failing.
- **Recent moves:** Okafor has quietly pulled three cold cases from the 1970s that contradict each other on geography — two crime scenes can't both be where the records say they are.

### Night
- **Status:** stable but tense
- **Key NPCs:**
  - Aldric Serrano — Vampire (Nosferatu, VtM) — operates out of a sealed level of the canal tunnels — wants to be left alone and is not being left alone — Status 3
  - Mireille Fontenot — Vampire (Toreador, VtM) — owns Maison Rouge gallery in the Fan — wants influence over the next generation of Kindred — Status 2
  - "The Shepherd" — Wolf (Werewolf: The Forsaken) — patrols the river corridor — wants to keep the spirit world sealed at Belle Isle — Status unknown
- **Current agenda:** Stabilize feeding territories disrupted by gentrification displacing the Bottom's night economy.
- **Recent moves:** Serrano and Mireille have not spoken in six months. Whatever broke between them is being felt as territory tension.

### Power
- **Status:** ascendant but fracturing internally
- **Key NPCs:**
  - Councilor Ingrid Voss — Power Status 3 — Silver Ladder senior — keeping secret that her Awakening was engineered, not natural — holds 1 Debt to Chris Caustes
  - Dr. Sebastian Olave — Mage (Mastigos, Mage: The Awakening) — Chair of VCU Philosophy Dept, unofficial Consilium Hierarch — wants to control the Overwrite investigation — Status 3
  - Nadia Vasek, 19 — Awakened ward of Chris Caustes — telekinetic and pyrokinetic, almost no control — Status 0
- **Current agenda:** Establish the Consilium's formal claim over investigating the Overwrite before another faction does.
- **Recent moves:** Olave has convened three closed faculty meetings in three weeks. No agenda distributed. Two junior Consilium members were not invited and are upset.

### Wild
- **Status:** fragmented
- **Key NPCs:**
  - Asha Tremblay — Fae (Changeling: The Lost, Fairest) — runs a curated vintage shop in the Fan called Threshold — wants to keep her Door to the Hedge accessible and secret — Status 2
  - Marcus Delacroix — street artist, mortal (Aware-adjacent) — sees the Overwrite happening in the city's visual record — wants someone to believe him — Status 0
  - "The Archivist" — identity and nature unknown — leaves annotated pages from historical documents at sites of Overwrite activity — possibly Fae, possibly something older — Status unknown
- **Current agenda:** The Hedge touching Richmond is shifting. Doors that used to open somewhere safe now open somewhere wrong.
- **Recent moves:** Asha had a visitor through the Hedge last week who claimed to be from "the prior version of the city." She has not told anyone.

## The Geography: Key Locations

**Shockoe Bottom** — One of the oldest parts of the city. Slave trade history, buried commerce. Construction keeps finding things. There is a fully intact street running beneath the current one — cobblestones, signage, a sealed entrance below. City records say it was excavated in 1987. The crew that supposedly did the work does not appear in any employment records.

**Hollywood Cemetery** — Massive, historic, overlooking the James. At least one grave has appeared with a recent burial date for someone still living. Groundskeeper Emmett Daye (40 years on the job) says the cemetery has added sections he doesn't remember being built. Borders Oregon Hill to the west.

**Belle Isle** — Small island in the James, former Civil War prison. Popular by day. At night, lights appear where no structures exist. Anyone who spends more than an hour there after dark loses time — fifteen minutes to three hours. The Shepherd patrols the river corridor here.

**Virginia State Capitol** — Designed by Jefferson. Archived legislative documents reference laws and events that never officially happened but seem to affect present-day governance. Okafor has found three such documents in the past month.

**The Canal System** — Underground, partially flooded, partially restored as a tourist walk. Aldric Serrano's territory begins where the tourist infrastructure ends. The tunnels go significantly deeper than any public record acknowledges.

**Creighton Court / East End** — Public housing community on the Henrico County border, built 1952, currently mid-redevelopment. Four of Richmond's six major housing complexes sit within one square mile here. Borders Oakwood Cemetery, which shows fresh flowers on graves empty for decades. The most concentrated zone of temporal displacement in the city outside Shockoe Bottom.

**Oregon Hill** — Promontory overlooking the James River and Belle Isle, Tredegar Iron Works below. Bordered by Hollywood Cemetery (west), VCU campus (north), Downtown Expressway (east). Industrial echoes strongest at shift-change hours: 6am, 2pm, 10pm. The Expressway occasionally does not exist.

## Lore & Rules of the World

- The Overwrite is real, active, and accelerating. Its origin is unknown. It appears centered beneath Shockoe Bottom, but this has not been confirmed.
- The city's appetite for self-mythology (Civil War ghosts, lost tunnels, hidden histories) acts as a Veil buffer. The supernatural can be explained as local color.
- Belle Isle loses time. The old prison records show similar anomalies. Whatever is happening there is old.
- The James River has a spirit presence. The Shepherd may be its ward, or may be something the river is keeping at bay.
- The Consilium (Power) has a formal but fragile agreement with the Night faction not to investigate each other's territories. Six months old. Already straining.
- The Silver Ladder in Richmond runs through VCU's institutional infrastructure. Faculty meetings are sometimes Consilium sessions. Grant applications are sometimes ritual budgets.
- The Overwrite's manifestation varies by neighborhood: buried history (Shockoe Bottom), temporal displacement (Creighton Court/East End), industrial echoes (Oregon Hill), artistic anomaly (The Fan), epistemological anomaly (The University), administrative confusion (Downtown).
- Player-to-player contact operates on a Tier system. Tier 2 interactions are asynchronous reaches between characters — one player frames intent, the other controls outcome, with a timed window before a soft miss fallback resolves it. This is not a crossover. See MC Instructions §interaction_system.
