## [2026-04-24] Something Moved in Shockoe Bottom

**Hubs:** Shockoe Bottom / The Fan / The University | **Circles:** Night · Wild · Power

A converted tobacco warehouse on the eastern edge of Shockoe Bottom was visited by at least one practitioner of note. The specifics of what was found there are not public knowledge, but the building registered on three separate informal monitoring channels — canal-adjacent anomaly reports, a disruption in the Overwrite's usual background hum at that address, and a brief flare of something in the Bottom's spirit ecology that the Shepherd, reportedly, did not cause and does not appear to be investigating. Whatever was inside the building before April 24th is no longer behaving as it was. Whether it is still there is a matter of some debate among those who track such things.

The name Maren Voss has resurfaced in two separate private inquiries within the same 24-hour window. Voss was a VCU graduate student in Folklore and Mythology who died approximately eight months ago under circumstances the Richmond Times-Dispatch summarized in six lines and did not follow up on. Her connection to events in Shockoe Bottom — and to the Overwrite's acceleration timeline — is not established in any public record. That two independent parties asked about her on the same day is either a coincidence or an indication that the city's self-rewriting has begun to surface something it previously buried. The Consilium has not commented. The Consilium has not been asked.

Threshold, the vintage shop on Ellwood in the Fan, closed early on the evening of April 24th. The proprietor, Asha Tremblay, was observed in what witnesses described as a "longer than social" conversation with two individuals before locking up — one adult, one young woman in her late teens. Asha's shop has been a point of quiet concern among those who track the Hedge's behavior near Monument Avenue. Her Hedge Door, which previously connected to stable territory, has been opening onto unfamiliar ground for three weeks. She has not disclosed whether the early closure was related.

The ley line under Monroe Park ran measurably off-tempo on April 24th. This is the third recorded instance in six weeks. The previous two were logged and filed by junior Consilium members who were subsequently not invited to the closed faculty meetings Dr. Sebastian Olave has been convening in Harris Hall. Whether the ley line's behavior is cause, effect, or coincidence is not established. Established: someone searched four names through a secure private channel on the evening of April 24th, and one of those searches was noticed by something that noticed back. The four names have not been publicly identified. The thing that noticed has not announced itself.

The James River is still running fast and dark. The dogwood bloom is still dead. Three weeks now, and whatever is pressuring the calendar has not named itself or slowed down. The city keeps adjusting its drafts. The people who pay attention are running out of ways to explain it as local color.
