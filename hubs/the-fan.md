# Hub — The Fan / Museum District

**Hub Type:** Wild (Dominant)

**Setting:** Victorian row houses line tree-shaded streets. Monument Avenue's Confederate statues watch over daily life (some removed, plinths remaining). The Virginia Museum of Fine Arts anchors the cultural scene. Mix of VCU students, young professionals, and old Richmond families. Trendy restaurants and art galleries. The Hedge grows thin where historical trauma meets artistic expression.

---

## The Overwrite Manifestation

**Overwrite Effect:** Artistic anomaly — works that shouldn't exist, Hedge thinning at monument scars.

**Active Anomalies:**
- Paintings and sculptures change subtly when no one's watching
- New artworks appear in galleries with no record of their creation or acquisition
- Monument plinths sometimes display different names or dates
- Street art appears and evolves without human intervention
- The Virginia Museum sometimes has exhibitions that don't match their official schedule

---

## Primary Faction

**Wild:** Dominant but fragmented. Artists, Changelings, and creative mortals form loose networks around specific venues or projects. The trauma of the monuments creates weak points where the Hedge bleeds through.

**Faction Status:**
- Wild: **2** (dominant, but scattered)
- Mortalis: **1** (students and young professionals)
- Power: **1** (museum board, old money families)
- Night: **1** (discrete influence in galleries and salons)

## Locations

### Galleries & Arts Venues
- **Maison Rouge:** Mireille's gallery and salon. Converted Victorian mansion. Hosts evening salons where art, politics, and supernatural business intertwine. The basement wine cellar has been... expanded.
- **Threshold:** Asha's vintage shop on Ellwood. Specializes in "authentic" pieces from different eras. The changing room is a Hedge Door (currently unsafe — opening onto unfamiliar ground for three weeks).
- **Virginia Museum of Fine Arts:** Major cultural institution. Vera Solis works in the conservation department. Has wings that sometimes appear on floor plans but don't exist in the building.
- **1708 Gallery:** Independent contemporary art space. Marcus sometimes shows his Overwrite documentation here, though the pieces tend to "evolve" after installation.

### The Monument Scars
- **Monument Avenue:** Former Confederate statues created wounds in reality when they were erected, wounds that widened when they were removed. The Hedge grows thin here.
- **Arthur Ashe Monument:** Stands in stark contrast to the Confederate legacy. Seems to anchor reality more firmly, but something watches from behind the tennis racket's strings.
- **The Empty Plinths:** Where removed monuments stood. Reality is thin here. Late at night, shapes that might be statues cast shadows.

### Residential
- **The Fan Historic District:** Rows of Victorian houses. Many converted to apartments for VCU students. Some houses have more rooms inside than their exterior should allow.
- **Canterbury:** Exclusive neighborhood. Old Richmond money. Where Power faction members live when they want to be close to Wild energy.

## Hub Moves

### Artistic Vision
**Trigger:** When you create or view art that captures something true about the Overwrite.
**Roll:** Spirit
- **10+:** Truth resonates. Choose 2: the work reveals a hidden detail about the Overwrite | it provides protection against reality distortion | it inspires others to resist the changes | it draws positive attention from your Circle
- **7-9:** Truth resonates but attracts attention. Choose 1 from above, but also mark corruption OR make an enemy.
- **Miss:** The Overwrite notices you noticing. Mark corruption and the MC makes a hard move.

### Gallery Opening
**Trigger:** When you leverage the Fan's artistic scene to make connections or gather information.
**Roll:** Heart
- **10+:** The scene embraces you. Put yourself in debt to a useful NPC and choose 2: learn something significant | make a valuable ally | acquire a useful resource | gain standing with a faction
- **7-9:** You're in, but it costs. Choose 1 from above.
- **Miss:** Social misstep with lasting consequences.

## Threats & Complications

- **Hedge Bleeds:** Reality is thin around the monument scars. Things from the Hedge sometimes slip through. The "Shepherd" wolf has been seen patrolling Monument Avenue.
- **Artistic Obsession:** The creative energy of the district can become consuming. Artists disappear into their work, sometimes literally.
- **Gentrification Pressure:** Old Fan families vs. young professionals vs. students. Tensions run high over development and authenticity.
- **Mireille's Influence:** The Toreador's salon is becoming a center of supernatural power, but her agenda remains unclear.

## Fragments & Beats

- Street art appears overnight that depicts events that haven't happened yet, or happened differently than people remember.
- The VMFA sometimes has "ghost exhibitions" — fully realized shows that appear on calendars and in databases but vanish if you try to visit them.
- Vera Solis's restorations are too perfect. She claims she's just "helping them become what they were meant to be," but some paintings now show different scenes than they originally depicted.
- The visitor from a "prior version of the city" that came through Asha's Hedge Door left behind items that don't belong to this reality. Asha has hidden them but isn't sure what to do with them.
- Monument Avenue's ghost statues can be seen in reflections, shadows, and peripheral vision. They show different figures than the Confederate generals who once stood there.
- Marcus's documentation project is a growing archive of "proof" that the city used to be different. Some of his pieces change themselves to match whatever the current reality becomes.
- The Tobacco Exchange Building that Mireille is buying has basement levels that don't appear on any official plans. Local historians insist it was never connected to the old canal system, but Mireille seems to think otherwise.
