# Hub — The University

**Hub Type:** Power (Dominant)

**Setting:** Virginia Commonwealth University (VCU) and Medical College of Virginia (MCV) campuses sprawl through midtown Richmond. Academic buildings, student housing, hospitals, and research facilities create a city within the city. The Consilium operates through academic networks, using scholarly legitimacy to pursue magical research. Old money families maintain influence through endowments and board positions.

---

## The Overwrite Manifestation

**Overwrite Effect:** Epistemological anomaly — impossible research results, contradictory academic records, knowledge that shouldn't exist.

**Active Anomalies:**
- Research papers cite sources that don't exist or contradict established facts
- Academic transcripts show degrees from institutions that were never founded
- Library databases contain books that were never published
- Laboratory experiments produce results that violate known principles
- Student records show enrollment dates that predate the university's founding
- Thesis defenses succeed despite completely impossible research claims

---

## Primary Faction

**Power:** Dominant through academic and medical institutions. The Consilium uses university resources for magical research while maintaining plausible academic cover. Medical center provides access to life/death mysteries.

## Locations

### Academic Buildings
- **Hibbs Hall:** Philosophy department headquarters. Dr. Olave's office occupies the entire top floor, which has more space inside than the building's exterior suggests.
- **Cabell Library:** Massive research library. Special Collections contains books that shouldn't exist. The basement levels extend deeper than official plans show.
- **Harris Hall:** Mathematics building. Prof. Tanaka's office overlooks Monroe Park. She has whiteboards covered in equations that describe impossible geometries. *Olave's closed faculty meetings are held here.*
- **Fine Arts Building:** Art history department has a private collection of Richmond artwork that shows different scenes depending on when you look at them.

### Medical District (MCV)
- **Main Hospital:** VCU Health's primary facility. Power faction uses medical access for research into life, death, and consciousness.
- **Medical Education Building:** Where Power faction members train new mages in the intersection of magic and medicine.
- **Research Labs:** Cutting-edge facilities that sometimes produce impossible results. The ethics review board has learned not to ask too many questions about certain projects.

### Consilium Sanctum
- **The Observatory:** Hidden facility built into the structure of multiple university buildings. Accessible through a network of maintenance tunnels and forgotten sub-basements. Contains the true Consilium library and ritual spaces.

### Student Life
- **Monroe Park:** Central campus green space. The ley line beneath the park has run measurably off-tempo three times in six weeks. Power faction monitors this closely.
- **The Commons:** Student center and gathering space. The Power faction monitors student population for potential Awakening candidates.

## Hub Moves

### Academic Authority
**Trigger:** When you use your institutional position to access restricted resources or information.
**Roll:** Mind
- **10+:** The institution serves you. Choose 2: you gain access to what you need | you avoid administrative scrutiny | you discover additional useful information | you establish lasting institutional advantage
- **7-9:** You get what you came for, but choose 1 from above.
- **Miss:** Red tape becomes a spider web. Mark corruption OR owe someone in administration a Debt.

### Peer Review
**Trigger:** When you present research or ideas to other academics to establish credibility or gain support.
**Roll:** Spirit
- **10+:** Your colleagues are convinced. Choose 2: you gain their active support | they provide additional resources | they share valuable information | your reputation is enhanced
- **7-9:** They're intrigued but cautious. Choose 1 from above.
- **Miss:** Academic politics turn against you. Make an enemy in your department.

## Threats & Complications

- **Consilium Politics:** Dr. Olave holds closed meetings that exclude some Power faction members. The hierarchy is shifting, and not everyone understands why. *(See arc-005)*
- **Awakening Program:** Councilor Marcus Voss runs a secretive program to engineer Awakenings in promising students. The ethical implications are massive, and the success rate is low. *(See arc-005)*
- **Academic Exposure:** Research into the Overwrite risks exposing magical reality through scholarly publication. The line between legitimate research and supernatural revelation is thin.
- **The Ley Line:** The ley line under Monroe Park has run measurably off-tempo three times in six weeks. Junior Consilium members who logged the anomalies were not invited to subsequent closed meetings.

## Fragments & Beats

- Dr. Olave's closed meetings happen in a conference room that has no official room number. The building directory lists it as "storage," but it's fully appointed for high-level discussions.
- Prof. Tanaka has been tracking mathematical patterns in Richmond's development that suggest the city's layout follows principles that weren't discovered until decades after the streets were laid out.
- The Consilium archives contain a collection of Richmond city plans that show streets, buildings, and neighborhoods that don't appear on any official maps.
- Medical students sometimes report seeing patients in the hospital who aren't on any official census. When they ask nurses or doctors, no one else remembers treating those people.
- The university's official founding date is 1968, but some graduation photos in the alumni office show ceremonies dating back to the 1940s.
- Research grants for certain projects are approved instantly despite lacking proper documentation. The funding comes from endowments that don't appear in the university's financial records.
- Dr. Olave's research into consciousness and perception has produced results that other universities have been unable to replicate. When pressed for details about methodology, he cites "institutional knowledge" that he won't share.
