# Hub — Oregon Hill

**Hub Type:** Mortalis (Dominant)

**Setting:** Perched on a hill overlooking the James River, Oregon Hill has been a working-class neighborhood since the 1840s when Tredegar Iron Works employees built cottages near the factory. Named by Irish canal workers who joked that moving so far from downtown felt like heading to Oregon Territory. The neighborhood sits wedged between Hollywood Cemetery, VCU campus, and the Downtown Expressway. Despite gentrification pressures, it maintains its blue-collar identity through a tight-knit community of longtime families, artists, and students.

---

## The Overwrite Manifestation

**Overwrite Effect:** Industrial echo — the neighborhood overlays different eras of labor and industry, with phantom work shifts and spectral manufacturing.

**Active Anomalies:**
- Factory whistles blow shift changes for mills that closed decades ago
- Residents wake to the sound of iron forges and smell of coal smoke from the valley below
- Pay stubs appear in mailboxes for work at defunct companies, sometimes with accurate wages for current bills
- Tredegar Iron Works sometimes appears fully operational with workers who don't recognize modern safety equipment
- Houses show foundations and extensions from different periods of construction simultaneously
- Hollywood Cemetery occasionally expands to include graves of people still living in the neighborhood

**Industrial echo strongest at shift-change hours: 6am, 2pm, 10pm.**

## Primary Faction

**Mortalis:** Dominant through working-class solidarity and neighborhood pride. Three generations often live within blocks of each other. Residents identify strongly with Oregon Hill's history and resist changes that threaten its character.

**Faction Status:**

## Locations

### Residential Core
- **The Historic District:** 19th-century worker cottages on narrow lots. Some houses show room additions from multiple time periods existing simultaneously.
- **Student Blocks:** Rental properties near VCU campus. Landlords sometimes collect rent for apartments that existed in previous configurations of the buildings.
- **The Old Families:** Long-term residents in houses passed down through generations. These families anchor the community's memory and cultural continuity.

### Community Anchors
- **St. Andrew's Episcopal Church:** Built with Grace Arents funding in 1894. Services sometimes include parishioners from different eras of the neighborhood's history.
- **William Byrd Community House:** Provides social services and meeting space. Programs occasionally serve participants who died years ago but continue attending.
- **Arents Free Library:** Small neighborhood library. Books sometimes include local histories that describe events before they happen.

### Work & Art Spaces
- **Byrd Park Overlook:** Scenic views of the James River where residents gather. Sometimes shows industrial landscape from the valley's manufacturing heyday.
- **The Art Collective:** Informal spaces where local artists work and display. Exhibitions sometimes include work by artists who haven't moved to the neighborhood yet.
- **Home Studios:** Many residents work from converted garages and basements. Musicians hear echoes of industrial rhythms in their compositions.

### Liminal Spaces
- **Hollywood Cemetery Border:** The neighborhood's western edge where residents walk among Civil War graves and famous burials. New graves occasionally appear for living residents.
- **Tredegar Overlook:** Views of the old iron works site below. Workers sometimes appear at the historical park, continuing shifts that ended 150 years ago.
- **The Expressway Cut:** Where the Downtown Expressway carved through the neighborhood in the 1970s. The road sometimes shows different routes or doesn't exist at all.

## Threats & Complications

- **Gentrification Pressure:** VCU expansion and downtown development increase property values, forcing out longtime families. The Overwrite sometimes shows future versions where Oregon Hill has lost its character entirely.
- **Student-Resident Tension:** Large groups of college renters change neighborhood dynamics. Some students unknowingly interact with phantom residents from different eras, causing confusion.
- **Industrial Hazards:** Phantom factory operations create real dangers — toxic fumes from non-existent mills, injuries from spectral machinery, exposure to historical work conditions.
- **Development Conflicts:** City planning sometimes references infrastructure or zoning from multiple time periods simultaneously, creating legal and practical complications.
