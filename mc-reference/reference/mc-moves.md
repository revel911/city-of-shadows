# MC Moves

## MC Basic Moves

- Inflict harm or corruption
- Surface a conflict, ancient or modern
- Put someone in danger
- Propose an opportunity with a cost
- Reveal a deal done in their absence
- Turn a move back on them
- Offer or claim a Debt owed
- Mobilize resources to shift the odds
- Paint the city in magical tones
- Lock down, exploit, or claim a place of power
- Tell the consequences and ask
- Activate their stuff's downside
- After every move: 'What do you do?'

## MC Circle Moves

| Circle | Moves |
|---|---|
| Mortalis | Adapt to the changing circumstances; Gather in numbers to confront a threat; Discover information that puts someone in danger; Remind someone of their mundane obligations |
| Night | Display an aggressive show of force; Threaten someone's interests or holdings; Claim territory from the weak or foolish; Make the best of a difficult situation |
| Power | Impose a cost for the greater good; Mystically foreshadow a conflict or challenge; Act in opposition to chaos or change; Snap up magical resources vulnerable or exposed |
| Wild | Challenge the PCs with alien expectations and traditions; Offer extraordinary assistance for a sticky price; Pull something from one realm into another; Escalate conflict for reasons mysterious or opaque |

## Instinct Die

Every roll in Urban Shadows is 2d6, but one of those dice is the Instinct Die (a different color from the regular die). If a roll results in a failure (6 or lower) AND the Instinct Die shows a 1, it triggers an Extreme Failure unique to each playbook.

> **Optional:** This is a supplement rule.

### Circle Themes

| Circle | Theme |
|---|---|
| Mortalis | Desperation — a moment of panic, rash action, or vulnerability. |
| Night | Hunger — an uncontrollable supernatural urge. |
| Power | Temptation — a lapse in control over magic or destiny. |
| Wild | Chaos — instinct or otherworldly nature taking over. |

### Implementation

1. Roll 2d6 as normal — one die is always the Instinct Die.
2. If the total is a failure (6 or lower) AND the Instinct Die rolls a 1, trigger an Extreme Failure.
3. The MC chooses (or the player suggests) an appropriate failure from their playbook's list.
4. This failure should shift the narrative significantly, making things worse in an unexpected way.

## Extreme Failures

### Mortalis — Desperation

#### The Aware

- **Fight or Flight** — You misinterpret a situation as a supernatural threat and escalate it dangerously.
- **Loose Lips** — You reveal crucial information to the wrong person.
- **Frozen in Fear** — You hesitate at a critical moment, allowing an enemy to act first.

#### The Hunter

- **Twitchy Trigger Finger** — You attack before confirming your target, hitting an unintended victim.
- **Exhausted** — You push yourself too hard — take -1 ongoing until you rest.
- **Reckless Pursuit** — You rush into danger alone, setting yourself up for an ambush.

#### The Sworn

- **Oathbreaker** — You involuntarily betray your order's principles, drawing suspicion or punishment.
- **Blind Loyalty** — You obey an order without question, even if it's clearly wrong.
- **Marked for Judgment** — Your enemies take notice, and someone makes a move against you.

### Night — Hunger

#### The Vamp

- **Blood Frenzy** — You must feed immediately, attacking the nearest mortal.
- **Hypnotic Slip** — You involuntarily use a vampiric power in public, exposing yourself.
- **Predator Instincts** — You mistake an ally for prey, causing tension or worse.

#### The Wolf

- **Rage Shift** — You transform uncontrollably and attack anyone nearby.
- **Pack Instincts** — You abandon the scene to track something more important to your primal nature.
- **Beast Takes Over** — You act without human reasoning, causing collateral damage.

#### The Spectre

- **Haunting Presence** — Uncontrolled poltergeist activity exposes you to the world.
- **Lost in the Veil** — You slip into the spirit world and lose control of when you return.
- **Unfinished Business** — An obsession from your past takes over, making you ignore the present.

### Power — Temptation

#### The Oracle

- **Overwhelming Vision** — You collapse, lost in a prophetic trance, missing vital moments.
- **Doom Foretold** — You blurt out a cryptic prophecy that disturbs everyone around you.
- **Cursed Sight** — You unwittingly make a prophecy come true in the worst way.

#### The Wizard

- **Spell Backlash** — A magical mishap harms an ally or warps reality in an unintended way.
- **Dark Whisperings** — A supernatural force tempts you toward corruption — acting on it earns you a Debt.
- **Arcane Drain** — Your power is temporarily depleted, preventing you from using Let It Out or spells for a scene.

#### The Tainted

- **Demonic Compulsion** — Your patron forces you to act in their interest, regardless of personal goals.
- **Infernal Aura** — Your true nature is revealed, terrifying mortals and alerting supernatural foes.
- **Unholy Craving** — You must indulge in an extreme vice immediately (violence, suffering, indulgence, etc.).

### Wild — Chaos

#### The Fae

- **Oathbreaker's Curse** — A past promise suddenly binds you, restricting your actions.
- **Glamour Gone Wrong** — Your illusion fails spectacularly, revealing something you didn't want exposed.
- **The Call of the Court** — You are compelled to abandon your current goal to serve fae interests.

#### The Imp

- **The Con Falls Apart** — You are caught in a lie at the worst moment.
- **Demonic Marker** — Someone realizes what you are and gains leverage over you.
- **The House Always Wins** — A past scheme backfires, putting you in immediate danger.

#### The Veteran

- **Old Wounds Resurface** — A past trauma distracts you, giving your enemies an advantage.
- **Debts Come Due** — Someone from your past makes a move, forcing you to owe them or suffer consequences.
- **No Easy Out** — You try to walk away, but the supernatural drags you back in violently.
