# Slasher — Slasher (Hunter: The Vigil supplement)

**Base Playbook (natural pairing):** Any Mortalis
**Prerequisite Move:** (see extension induction rules below)
**Key Mechanics:** Ripper Undertakings define the Slasher's method and legend; compatible with all Mortalis Circle archetypes; expanded Advancement Chart.

---

SLASHER — ADD-ON RULES

## Information

Here you will find the Slasher add-on rules and text for use with any of the Mortalis archetypes associated with Urban Shadows. You can expect to find a new Advancement Chart for your mortal characters (Corruption advancements are unaltered) and 5 Ripper Undertakings to start you down your path of murder and madness.

Note: Slashers created with any of the Mortalis Circle Urban Shadows archetypes do not automatically start with an Undertaking. It is up to the group and MC to decide if you want to start with an Undertaking; if so, you give up your beginning archetype Move at the start of the game.


## Ripper Undertakings
(Only able to choose one)


### Avenger

Avengers are driven by the crimes and pains they have suffered. Usually an Avenger starts with something very specific to punish, such as the murder of a loved one or a death due to an incompetent surgeon. They find the person or people responsible and slaughter them, sometimes one by one, sometimes in an orgy of carnage. It might end there — but if they cannot move on, the call to murder becomes their passion. Avengers do not necessarily become indiscriminate killers, but the line blurs as they become more willing to commit murder for other reasons. They move further away from their original purpose, potentially taking more cruel and expedient paths to success. As this happens, stories of their exploits spread, and they grow closer to becoming a Legend.

Trait: Your drive to kill is your defining purpose. While you can function in normal life, once you identify someone as a target, they dominate your focus.
- Once per session, when you make progress toward eliminating a marked target, mark Experience.
- You are the stuff of whispered legend. Every feared figure has a weakness. When you reveal your personal weakness to an enemy (not an ally), mark Experience.

[MOVE] Working the Room: Avengers might try to kill their victims one by one, but sooner or later they wind up with a roomful of people that require their attention. You must be prepared for the ability to kill an entire room and hopefully live to continue your mission. When you Turn to Violence against multiple opponents in a confined space, roll with Blood. On a 10+, hold 3. On a 7-9, hold 2. On a miss, you underestimate them; MC makes a hard move.
Spend 1 hold to:
- Gain +1 armor against an attack.
- Increase your next Turn to Violence harm by one step.

[MOVE] Stand Firm: You cannot falter. Whatever drives you to kill, you must see it through. When you Keep Your Cool or Let It Out while directly pursuing your mission, take +1 forward, unless your personal weakness is exploited.

[MOVE] Unnatural Sense: You have a psychic sense that surpasses most of the populace when it comes to finding those who need to be met with vengeance. At the beginning of a session, roll with Spirit. On a 10+, hold 3. On a 7-9, hold 1. On a miss, your instincts are clouded; the MC tells you why.
Spend 1 hold to:
- Ask one extra question on Figure Someone Out or Study a Place of Power.
- Ask: "Is there anywhere nearby I can continue my mission?"

[MOVE] Strength from the Tales: You have legends being told about you to frighten little children into obedience, or to make people think twice before walking down that one alley or into the woods. When you meet someone important (your call), roll with Spirit. On a hit, they have heard of you — say what they know. On a 10+, take +1 forward when dealing with them.
If you have Status 3+ from the Mortalis Circle, when someone hears of your legend secondhand, their mind fills in the gaps with exaggerated tales, making it easier to manipulate their expectations. Take +1 forward on all Persuade or Mislead rolls involving mortals who already know of you.

[MOVE] Unshakeable: Your legend and mission drive you. It is this very drive which leads people to fear you, survivors and crime scenes alike spreading tales of your name. When you kill an enemy and make an example of the slain, nearby enemies become frightened of you.

[MOVE] Right Behind You: (requires Unnatural Sense) You know your own legend so well that you gain an almost supernatural sense. You know when and where the sinful actions you detest are occurring, and as a result you can make those who have committed them jump at shadows like a horror movie killer. When you Let It Out, add this option:
- Instantly travel to a place where your legend is feared, fully armed.


### Brute

The Brute might seem simple, even stupid. Maybe they are. However, Brutes often retain enough intelligence to have a sense of deliberation about their hunts. They change clothes and tactics as necessary to avoid detection. To achieve their superlative skill at taking life, they have given up everything else — cognition, social skills, even personality. But that is the source of the Brute's horror. They cannot be reasoned with or intimidated. They drive the knife home, watch the light in their victim's eyes die, and then search, slowly and methodically, for the next one. Some Brutes lead double lives, remaining human most of the time but picking up their weapons when the full moon rises, when the sun sets, on the third Sunday of every month, or just when the mood strikes them. As they kill more people, they become more detached, less human, until only the Mask remains.

Trait: Not exactly the brightest killer, you nevertheless cannot be swayed from your attempts to kill a target.
- You cannot be persuaded or reasoned with once you have committed to an attack. When someone tries to persuade you to abandon a kill and you refuse, mark Experience.
- When you knowingly walk into obvious danger with bloodlust in your heart, mark Experience.

[MOVE] Unstoppable: You are not just a killing machine — you can take hits better than anyone. Years of physical conditioning have left your nerve endings feeling little to no pain. Gain +1 natural armor.

[MOVE] Olympic Strength: You are as strong as most bodybuilders and Olympic athletes, the result of hours of dedication and training mixed with an increased amount of adrenaline. When you use brute force to break or move an obstacle, roll with Blood. On a 10+, choose 3. On a 7-9, choose 2. On a miss, you fail or cause unintended consequences.
- It does not take long.
- Nothing valuable is damaged.
- It is quiet.
- It can be easily repaired.

[MOVE] Brutal Killer: Like the name of your undertaking, you do not hold back when attacking in melee. Once you start, you have no restraint and cannot stop until the task is complete. When you Turn to Violence with a melee weapon, inflict +1 harm.

[MOVE] Unstoppable Killing Machine: (requires Unstoppable) Some killers are good. Unlike them, you are a true monster in a fight. Whether they are running or trying to fight back, it is completely futile. When you enter a violent situation, roll with Blood. On a 10+, hold 3. On a 7-9, hold 2. On a miss, hold 1 but take -1 forward.
Spend 1 hold to:
- Instantly kill, disable, or disarm an NPC.
- Redirect an attack.
- Close distance on a target.
- Intercept an attack for an ally.
- Ignore all damage for one hit.
- Increase your attack's harm by +1.

[MOVE] Armor Crusher: (requires Olympic Strength) You can focus your strength into an attack that penetrates armor — or maybe you just hit that hard. Either way, your attacks ignore all armor from non-supernatural sources.

[MOVE] Against the World: You are a monster to fight. Even groups of attackers fear you as they find that numbers do not matter against you. When you fight a group, treat them as a single opponent.


### Charmer

The Charmer is deadly not because they are strong or even smart — they kill with trust. They are impeccably polite, sympathetic, well-spoken, and knowledgeable. They choose their victims, seduce them at least to the point of intimacy, and then the victim feels the knife slide home. A Charmer might troll for victims in any number of ways: finding an isolated community and murdering and replacing some important figure, bar-hopping every night while developing a reputation as a true gentleman who will give you a ride home if you are too drunk to drive and will never take advantage. Charmers are ready to pull up stakes at any moment, but they prefer to keep their charade going as long as possible. The goal for them is not necessarily the kill — that is something they do almost in spite of themselves. What a Charmer loves most is that their victims trust them.

Trait: You can get into any social scene; velvet ropes and money are no obstacle to you.
- You can gain access to any social setting.
- When you mark multiple Debts to gain access to an exclusive place, mark Experience.
- If someone turns you down and you kill them that session, mark Experience.

[MOVE] Disarming: You are a natural at getting people to trust you, right up until the point that you kill them. When you spend time earning someone's trust, roll with Heart. On a 10+, hold 3. On a 7-9, hold 2. On a miss, they see through you; MC makes a hard move.
Spend 1 hold to:
- Have them give you something.
- Have them act as your spy.
- Have them defend you.
- Have them follow a simple order.
In addition, take +1 when you Hit the Streets.
If you have Status 2+ in the Mortalis Circles, mortals rationalize their trust in you even when it makes no logical sense. When you roll 7-9, you may spend 1 Debt owed to you to avoid suspicion, causing the target to justify their trust in you to others.

[MOVE] Strength of Personality: You are charismatic. While not supernaturally so, it is still enough to accomplish most of what you want when it comes to people. When you persuade an NPC or cash in a Debt, any PC who does not do things your way takes -1 forward for the evening. You take +1 forward against any NPC that does not do things your way.

[MOVE] Break Their Trust: You get close to people and then finally kill when they think you are the closest person to them. That often requires taking a chance so they do not see it coming too soon. When you betray someone and kill them, roll with Heart. On a 10+, choose 1. On a 7-9, choose 1 but take 1 harm.
- Kill an NPC.
- Inflict a Scar on a PC (if they have no Scars left, they die).
- Immobilize a target.

[MOVE] Deadly Distraction: Sometimes you put people on edge just by being near them. But for a moment, you can make them forget about all of that and do one thing you say — make a guard not notice the body in the back seat, or invite someone into the back room. If you are about to be found out, you can Let It Out and make them perform a simple task or forget a short and very recent memory. On a 10+, it works cleanly. On a 7-9, someone else sees you or sees what you are trying to hide.

[MOVE] Manipulator: As a charmer, you are a natural socialite. You know humans, you know how to motivate them, and you know how to manipulate them into doing what you want. Whenever you persuade someone, treat a 7-9 as a 10+ and a miss as a 7-9. On a miss, the MC still makes a hard move.

[MOVE] Heart to Heart: You have such Heart and charisma that it is almost unnatural, getting people to open up to you. To get someone talking, you usually have to give up something in return. When you speak frankly with someone, you can ask their player a question from the list below. They must answer it truthfully, then they may ask you a question from the list (which you must also answer truthfully).
- Whom do you serve?
- What do you wish I would do?
- How can I get you to ____?
- What are you really feeling right now?
- What do you most desire?


### Freak

The Freak is similar to the Brute, but rather than being defined by strength and tenacity, the Freak's characteristic trait is deformity. They shun humanity, sticking to dark places and covering themselves with heavy clothes or scarves. They hate other people for the way they have been treated, and this hatred ultimately serves as the impetus to kill. Of all of the Undertakings, Freaks are the most likely to engage in cannibalism, necrophilia, or other especially depraved activities with their victims. Their methods of murder vary but are often horribly bestial — Freaks attack with teeth, claws, and improvised weaponry. Freaks are also more likely to form attachments to others of their kind, and it is not uncommon for Freaks to gather in small clans such as a travelling circus.

Trait: You are freakishly deformed in some aspects. This deformity, however, also has a beneficial aspect.
- Your monstrous form grants you advantages but draws scorn.
- Once per session, when you are publicly humiliated for your appearance, mark Experience.

[MOVE] Lay of the Land: You know your way around unusually well, even in ways that others cannot. You know how to obtain things. Gain +1 on attempts to Hit the Streets when gathering resources.

[MOVE] Revulsion: You are spurned and beaten by the common man and by society, and you know how to hold a grudge. Whenever you take a scar, name the person you hold most responsible. Take +1 ongoing to all rolls versus them, forever. Rolls with them directly as a target count, as do rolls against their family, friends, minions, or property at MC discretion.

[MOVE] The Lepers: You know a small group of people like yourself — deformed. While they may not be killers, they follow the same code of outcasts standing together and are willing to back you up within reasonable limits. You join a [small] group.

[MOVE] Freak of Nature: You have mutated into something hideously inhuman. Whether you grow horns, claws, thick leathery skin, or something else entirely, there is no hiding what you are. Describe your new form to the MC. Some form aspects may grant bonuses such as natural weapons and armor; these bonuses should be determined at MC discretion.

[MOVE] Dreadful Mien: Your hideousness drives people toward reckless flight, triggering the primitive centers of the human brain. Most humans can clamp down on this instinctive response and remain in your presence, but not all of them. When someone sees your true form, roll with Heart. On a 10+, choose 1. On a 7-9, the MC chooses 1.
- They freeze in fear.
- They flee in terror.

[MOVE] At Home Anywhere: Humans instinctively build their cities in a consistent fashion, even if each location and geographic region differs. Resources are found in the same general locations and you know where. Treat all Hit the Streets rolls as one step higher.


### Genius

Geniuses are Slashers that kill with their minds. They profile their targets and tailor their methods of abduction and murder to suit them. They think several steps ahead of their victims, predicting what a target will do based on their predispositions and presenting a set of options, then sit back and watch their victims kill themselves. It is not uncommon for Geniuses to kill using traps rather than doing the deed themselves. Some consider killing coarse and vulgar, feeling that by allowing victims the possibility of Escape it negates their moral responsibility in the victims' deaths. Geniuses tend to be technically skilled, with or without training — given a few hours and the proper tools, they can pull apart a car, figure out how it works, and put it back together, probably rigged with a trap. They still play cat and mouse games with their victims, making allusions to the victim's eventual fate while keeping a civil tongue.

Trait: The Genius kills targets with their intellect, playing mind games and luring enemies into traps. They cannot relate to those they consider beneath them.
- When you Mislead someone to set up a later kill, mark Experience.
- If your plan fails and you must share it with another, mark Experience.

[MOVE] Profiling: By taking a few minutes to study someone, you learn more about them than you know about yourself. When you spend time analyzing someone, take +1 ongoing against them. If you roll 12+ against them, mark corruption.

[MOVE] Unconquerable Challenge: (requires Profiling) Your plots are often multi-pronged assaults relying on backup plans that lead to overkill. When a person you have profiled is about to defeat an obstacle you have placed before them, roll with Mind. On a hit, they overcome the obstacle and are immediately faced with something new you had as a backup. On a 10+, the new obstacle appears before they have beaten the current one. On a miss, the new obstacle backfires and your victim gains their freedom.

[MOVE] Trap Master: You like to lull your victims into a false sense of security, setting up a web to capture them. When you set a trap, roll with Mind. On a 10+, choose 3 effects and 1 drawback. On a 7-9, choose 2 effects and 2 drawbacks. On a miss, the trap misfires.
Effects:
- Deal 2 harm (can be chosen multiple times).
- Slow the target (-1 to Escape; choosing this a second time immobilizes them).
- Trap is destructive (+messy).
- Trap affects an area (+range).
Drawbacks:
- You get caught in the trap.
- It is extremely loud.
- It is easy to spot.
- It is easy to disarm.

[MOVE] Compelling Madness: (requires Profiling) Your insanity infects those around you, to the point that you can turn even your most hated enemy into a loyal minion with enough work. You must first have profiled this person. When you attempt to Persuade or Mislead that person, roll with Mind. On a 10+, treat it as a 10+ Persuade or Mislead. On a 7-9, treat it as a 7-9. On a miss, the MC makes a hard move.

[MOVE] Vector: As a Genius, you plan things meticulously and precisely, calculating angles and vectors when laying your traps. Anytime you or a trap you lay attacks an area, you can exempt specific people from the damage entirely, even when they would otherwise be hit.

[MOVE] Minions: You force people to do your will from behind a wall of shadows. Sometimes your minions do not even realize they are as such, sometimes they are entirely willing, and other times you strong-arm them with proper leverage. When you wish to have your minions do something, tell the MC. This move functions like a workspace, only with people as your tools.
