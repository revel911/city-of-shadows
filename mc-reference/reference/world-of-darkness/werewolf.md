# Werewolf — Werewolf: The Forsaken

**Base Playbook (natural pairing):** The Wolf
**Prerequisite Move:** (none explicit — natural shifter)
**Key Mechanics:** Auspices tied to moon phases grant moves; Tribes grant moves and gang benefits; expanded Advancement Chart.

---

## Information

Here you will find the Werewolf the Forsaken add-on rules and text for the Wolf archetype associated with Urban Shadows. You can expect to find a new Advancement Chart increasing advancements of your character (Corruption advancements are unaltered), 5 Auspices tied to one of five phases of the moon, and 5 Werewolf Tribes.

Note: Werewolves created with the Urban Shadows Wolf archetype do not automatically start with an Auspice. It is up to the group and MC to decide if you want to start within an Auspice; if so, you give up your beginning archetype Move at the start of the game.


## Auspices
(Only able to choose one)

Each Werewolf has an Auspice, a traditional role in Werewolf society tied to one of five phases of the moon. Luna decides each Werewolf's Auspice based on their personality and abilities; accordingly, a Werewolf will go through the First Change during the corresponding moon phase. The five Auspices are:


### Rahu
The Full Moon

The Rahu auspice is technically a blessing, but few Werewolves look on it as wholly benign, for the gift of the full moon is mad fury. This auspice is filled with warriors of all kinds, from brawlers to seasoned commanders.

**Trait:** There are few Werewolves as honest with one another as Rahu. Being able to read one another's strength tends to strip away some of the nonsense that other warrior fraternities can accumulate. Once per session, when you proclaim your intentions of violence toward a stronger individual, mark Experience. You are also responsible for the combat skills of the whole pack and should be encouraged to help train other pack members. Once per session, if you help in the training of your pack or allies, mark Experience.

Moves for Rahu

[MOVE] Rage Armor: The natural resilience that the Forsaken have inherited makes Werewolves daunting foes. When you roll 12+ on Turn to Violence, you can choose to mark corruption to receive +2 armor against all enemies in the current scene.

[MOVE] Battle Mind: Once per session, you enter a mental state that is difficult to influence with powers that affect mental control. While Battle Mind is active, take -1 to all non-violent moves.

[MOVE] Righteous Rage: You have learned to direct the rage that makes you a monster, even if you do not wish to control it outright. When you Transform and do not choose to keep your sanity, you never attack allies unless they attack you first. You still attack everything else, but your allies are safe from you. Due to the lack of sane thought and control, you are unable to end your transformation at will.


### Cahalith
The Gibbous Moon

Equal parts priest and historian, oracle and war-howler, the Cahalith is the voice of Luna.

**Trait:** Cahaliths are meant to keep legends and old tales alive, whether to teach or inspire. When you witness an ally's death, you can compose a tale about it emphasizing the ally's deeds and skill. When used to inspire others, you and all allies you tell the tale to mark Experience. Cahaliths must find an outlet for the visions Luna sends them, lest they go mad. If you do not act upon your dreams by the next moon phase, you become increasingly more insane as your dreams grow more intense. When you are given a task from Luna through your dream state and complete that task, mark Experience.

Moves for Cahalith

[MOVE] Unspoken Communication: Packmates visible to the Cahalith during activation are joined in mental communication. When you share Status 2 or higher with the Night Circle, you and your packmates may communicate telepathically for a scene.

[MOVE] Rallying Cry: By giving of yourself and howling of past glories, you can give packmates hope even in their darkest hour. When someone comes to you for advice, tell them what the best course of action is. If they do it, they take +1 ongoing on the task and you mark 1 experience.

[MOVE] Soul-Singing: To be a Cahalith is to be ridden by one's dreams, to sense things in sharp context, to have one's soul constantly churning with thoughts and impulses that demand expression. Within your territory, you always have a foreboding sixth sense that allows you quick glimpses of the future. Once per scene, if you fail a roll within your territory, re-roll.


### Elodoth
The Half Moon

The half moon hangs in balance, and balance is Luna's gift to the Elodoth. The Elodoth are judges and executioners, able to weigh both sides of any argument and ideally suited to act as voice of the law.

**Trait:** Elodoths have the greatest duty to make certain that all pack members are receiving their fair due — their fair share of the spoils, their fair allotment of Essence, their fair chance to serve the pack as suits their specialty. You rule your territory and pack with an iron fist. When you give orders that are just despite knowing the bad consequences to come, mark Experience. Elodoths may also have to serve as the one who passes sentence on a packmate who violates the Oath or otherwise does something to the detriment of the pack, balancing justice with necessity. Once per session, if you pass judgment on a packmate who has violated the oaths of the tribe, mark Experience.

Moves for Elodoth

[MOVE] Echo Dream: You can sense psychic impressions upon an area or place via touch. Information comes in the form of symbolic visions about the most significant event involving the target. When you Let It Out to glean insight from an area, you may ask the following in addition to the standard options:
- What is going to happen here?
- What has happened here?
- How am I involved with this location?

[MOVE] Aura of Truce: When you use this power, a subtle half-moon halo appears in the air behind you, bathing everyone present in calming, soothing light. When you pass judgment on someone to end a feud, you may persuade with the force of the spirits; roll with Spirit.

[MOVE] Truth-Seeker: Elodoths are the judges and arbiters among the Forsaken. Knowing the truth is a crucial aspect of this role. Whenever another character tells you something, you may freely ask "Is this person telling the truth?" as if you had spent a hold from Figure Someone Out.


### Ithaeur
The Crescent Moon

The crescent moon is the auspice of the shaman, the speaker with spirits and the spirit hunter. Yet, as the crescent moon is just enough light to count as moonlight, the Ithaeur possesses just enough knowledge to get into trouble.

**Trait:** Just as all Werewolves are designed for combat, so too are they all able to interact with the spirit world. The Ithaeur has a greater understanding of the hierarchies and relationships of the various spirits within the pack's territories and at least some knowledge of where to start looking in the Shadow for solutions to spiritual problems. Once per session, when others are in trouble and you commune with the Spirits to solve your ally's issues, mark Experience. They also know best how to defend themselves and their packmates against the advances of hostile spirits, as well as how to take the fight to a spirit enemy. Whenever you build a totem or ward for an ally or packmate with the intent of protecting them against spirits, mark Experience.

Moves for Ithaeur

[MOVE] Shadow-Speaking: This opens the ability to have contact with spirits, allowing willing spirits on the other side to speak through your voice. Chatty spirits may come willfully, or when you Hit the Streets looking specifically for answers from those less inclined to talk.

[MOVE] Between the Weave: Once per session, when you or an ally suffer harm, roll with Spirit. On a 10+, negate the harm. On a 7-9, negate the harm but gain a Debt to the spirit.

[MOVE] Wisdom of Patience: Ithaeur are meant to be masters of wisdom, and what better way to show it than the knowledge of patience. When you wait on an action at cost (giving an enemy time to attack, or watching to see what a gang member does next; MC's discretion), you automatically succeed as if you had rolled a 10+. If the move is advanced, you succeed as if you had rolled a 12+.


### Irraka
The New Moon

Those who change under the new moon are strange and unpredictable. Luna's blessing to the Irraka is the ability to walk unseen, the gift of deception. Irraka are scouts, spies, and trackers.

**Trait:** An Irraka is the silent hunter of the pack, the one who leads when it is time to hunt quietly and kill swiftly. You are the one with the gifts to sneak into places where you are not welcome, to learn secrets and stir things up. Once per session, when you can complete a task using stealth, mark Experience. This sometimes means splitting away from the pack to accomplish your scouting or stalking. Once per session, if you abandon your allies to stalk prey or to scout further ahead to gain valuable information, mark Experience.

Moves for Irraka

[MOVE] Short Cuts: Your senses and knowledge help you find the fastest paths to get somewhere. When you come across an obstacle in your path, roll with Mind, or when you Let It Out or Keep Your Cool, roll with Spirit. On a 10+, you spot a way around the obstacle. On a 7-9, you spot a path but there is a complication (MC's call). On a miss, you must face the obstacle head-on.

[MOVE] Deep Pockets: Irraka are cunning and meticulous; you can conceal an item from scrutiny of all senses. When you hide the item and keep it within close distance, roll with Mind. On a hit, the item remains hidden from all senses of others. On a 7-9, the power lasts until the object is touched or moved.

[MOVE] Blend In: You blend into the environment. You are not invisible or chameleonic, but simply do not attract attention. The effect ends if you do anything notable like attacking or kicking in doors, otherwise lasting for a scene. Talking does not end the effect and security measures like retina scanners are not fooled. The gift does not work if there is a lack of bystanders to blend with.


## Tribes
(Only able to choose one)

Joining a Werewolf Tribe confers not only your Tribe's gifts but also the group/gang benefit at the same time.

The Forsaken have five Tribes. These Tribes consist of Werewolves who choose to follow one of five ancient and powerful wolf-spirits called the Firstborn — the first born children of Father Wolf (but not of Mother Luna) who have allied with the Forsaken. Every Tribe demands that the Werewolves who belong to it act in accordance with a certain ban. The five Tribes are:


### Blood Talons

The Tribe of Fenris Wolf promotes the warrior and wolf aspects of the Werewolf. Their tribal ban is "Offer no surrender that you would not accept." Matching warrior ethos with a religious kinship to their ferocious totem, the Blood Talons are creatures as much of the battlefield as of the hunt. To the Blood Talon way of thinking, the most serious problems must be solved permanently, and the greatest tools they have to do so are their fangs. Those drawn to the Tribe have a strong tendency to be competitive and aggressive, even before the First Change. Members were often adversarial during their school years and later proved too combative to succeed in most lines of work. Still others found lines of work in which their aggression served them well.

Moves for Blood Talons

[MOVE] The Right Words: Sometimes keeping a pack in line or a soldier's morale up hinges on knowing just what to say. When your gang fights for you, roll with Heart. On a 10+, hold 3. On a 7-9, hold 1. Over the course of the fight, spend your hold 1 for 1 to make your gang:
- Make a hard advance.
- Stand strong against a hard advance.
- Make an organized retreat.
- Show mercy to their defeated enemies.
- Fight and die to the last.
On a miss, your gang could turn on you or try to hand you over to your enemy.

[MOVE] Victor's Song: All is lost when heart is lost. Yet even when hope has vanished, newfound courage can claim victory where there was only defeat. In the face of hopeless odds — whether surviving a suicide charge or bracing a crumbling levee — Victor's Song does not push allies beyond their limits; it pushes their limits beyond what they thought possible. You must howl, chant, or sing a song extolling your comrades to valor and courage. Roll with Heart. On a 10+, receive both. On a 7-9, choose 1.
- Allies find themselves quicker and sturdier (+1 armor for the session).
- +1 ongoing for the next scene for all allies within earshot.
On a miss, the spirits are offended by the clumsy use of their gift. You lose the ability to use any of your Tribe's gifts for the scene.

[MOVE] Crushing Blow: Sometimes a Werewolf chooses to kill without using their claws or fangs. You gain +1 harm when using any melee weapon or fighting unarmed.

[MOVE] Legendary Arm: (requires Crushing Blow) A Werewolf has tremendous strength to crush their enemies or hurl tremendous and unwieldy objects great distances. When you show a display of power in battle, roll with Blood. On a 10+, choose 2. On a 7-9, choose 1. On a miss, mark corruption and the MC makes a hard move.
- You count as a force of nature.
- When you damage an enemy in melee, you can root them in place through the fiction. As long as you do not engage another opponent, they cannot leave their position.
- +1 harm done to enemies this scene.
- Your opponent is unnerved by your presence and becomes Scared.
- You are able to lift objects that weigh as much as a ton.
- You can spend your second option to upgrade the weight limit up to 10 tons.

[MOVE] Leach Rage: A Werewolf literally steals the power of a rival and adds it to their own. With a hissing inhalation, you draw off your opponent's power in a wash of energy that distorts the air between you. When you look to steal the life force of your victim, roll with Blood. On a hit, you may heal 1 harm if you injure your opponent in this scene. On a 10+, heal 2 harm. On a 7-9, heal 1 harm. On a miss, the move is turned back on you. If the victim is from the Mortalis or Night Circle, mark corruption.
If you have Status 3+ in the Night Circle, when you use Victor's Song or Leach Rage, roll with Blood. On a 10+, your abilities extend to two additional allies or let you drain an extra point of harm from your enemy. On a 7-9, you gain your normal effect but must either mark corruption or take -1 forward due to the intensity of your transformation.


### Bone Shadows

Occultists who seek to restore relations with the Spirit Courts under the guidance of their patron, Death Wolf. Their tribal ban is "Pay each Spirit in kind." Bone Shadow legends speak of Kamduis-Ur, the Death Wolf, the quietest of Father Wolf's pups, and the hunt that encircled the Earth. Many Werewolves who become Bone Shadows have experiences with the spirit world or the supernatural in general before their First Change. They notice signs that there is more to the world than their peers recognize. While what is hidden might horrify them, they are compelled to investigate.

Moves for Bone Shadows

[MOVE] Ward Versus Predators: This gift lets other predators know that a tract of land is the territory of a Werewolf. When you return to the place you live, you will always know if another predator has been there. Any PC wanting to get into your place must Study a Place of Power successfully to bypass the ward, while NPCs will not even attempt it.

[MOVE] Sense Malice: Hatred has its own scent. When you lend a hand or get in the way, roll with Mind instead of roll+Circle. You can also sense the presence of unusually strong aggression, hatred, or anger.

[MOVE] Scent of Taint: The smell of supernatural influence emanates from beings that are beyond human and mundane. At the beginning of the session, roll with Blood. On a 10+, hold 2. On a 7-9, hold 1. At any time, you or the MC can spend your hold to have you at the scene of a supernatural conflict (a real battle, not intimate violence between a small number of people).

[MOVE] Word of Quiet: A complicated word in the First Tongue that bears great power. The sound was allegedly spoken by the first Incarna of Death, and a portion of that great spirit's authority lingers in its twisted syllables. Once per session, when spoken, the word tugs at the hearts of all living beings that hear it, brushing them with the stillness of the grave. All living creatures capable of hearing the word suffer 1 harm (ap). If they are already in fear of your character or within your territory, they are also stunned. When stunned, they are unable to take any physical action for a short time. If they do take a physical action, the MC can make a hard move.
If you have Status 4+ in the Night Circle, when you use Corpse Witness or Word of Quiet, roll with Spirit. On a 10+, your invocation lingers, extending the effect to others in the area. On a 7-9, your power works as normal but the silence or knowledge you invoke carries a price — the dead may ask for something in return. If used in a sacred or haunted place, this ability automatically succeeds without a roll.

[MOVE] Corpse Witness: Many occultists believe that a corpse retains some memory of the moments just before its death. When you call a spirit back to its body, roll with Spirit. On a hit, ask it three of the following questions. On a 10+, it answers truthfully and directly. On a 7-9, it answers with vague impressions.
- How did you die?
- Where did you die?
- What killed you?
- Who were you in life?
- Where are you now?
- What do you still want done?


### Hunters in Darkness

Protectors of loci, the spiritual centers of the world. Black Wolf is their totem. Their tribal ban is "Let no sacred space in your territory be violated." The Hunters in Darkness concern themselves primarily with the places farthest from the encroaching effects of humanity. They mark out large tracts of territory in the pristine wilderness, tending the sacred places and minding the spirits endemic to those environs. Those Werewolves who become Hunters in Darkness are strongly self-reliant before the First Change and jealously protective of what they claim as theirs. Many are withdrawn and somewhat haughty, hoarding and hiding what is important to them without ever really questioning why. They have trouble trusting their peers and relatives, subconsciously valuing no one's opinions or judgments above their own.

Moves for Hunters in Darkness

[MOVE] Shadow Flesh: This gift affects the Werewolf's form, making them a living shadow. While transformed to shadowy matter, you can slip through areas with great ease, even sliding under doors or through cracks as long as there is sufficient light and contrast to remain in that form. Your body becomes a two-dimensional shadow of the same height and width that lies flat against the ground, walls, trees, and other surfaces. Any PC trying to Figure You Out does so at 1 step lower while you are in this form.

[MOVE] Blending: An expert hunter knows the value of not being seen. When you hide yourself from others, roll with Mind. On a 10+, both. On a 7-9, choose 1.
- You get in or around.
- You go unnoticed.
On a miss, neither.

[MOVE] Shadow Step: This gift allows a Werewolf to slip from one patch of shadow to another, confusing prey or predators as to their location. Though hardly the fastest means of getting around, slipping between shadows allows the Werewolf to move without being seen. When you move between shadows, roll with Blood and mark Wild. On a 10+, you transport yourself through the shadows to far distances, landing exactly on target, arriving silently and hidden. Additionally, choose one:
- You catch a whisper of the Wild — the shadows reveal a secret about something hidden in the area.
- You briefly phase into the Wild realm — Spirits and fae creatures take note of your passing.
- You become part of the darkness — for the rest of the scene, you appear blurred and indistinct, making it harder for others to track you (+1 ongoing when sneaking).
On a 7-9, you transport yourself through shadows to close distances but land off the mark. Choose one:
- You remain hidden, but something follows you from the Wild.
- You arrive unnoticed, but the shadows demand a price — a brief, involuntary vision of the Wild that may be disturbing or enlightening.
- Your passage distorts reality briefly, alerting supernatural entities sensitive to the Wild's touch.
On a miss, the Wild rejects your control. Choose one:
- You are trapped in the shadows, struggling to break free.
- You stumble out into an exposed area, completely visible.
- The Wild twists your destination, dropping you somewhere dangerous or somewhere you should not be.
If you have Status 2+ in the Night Circle, when you use Shadow Step, the shadows momentarily conceal your scent; supernatural trackers take -1 forward when attempting to follow your trail.

[MOVE] Nature's Vengeance: When attacked in the forest or any area with a decent amount of foliage (MC's discretion), nature comes to your call, attacking all who oppose you. The summoned gang is treated as a small 2 harm, 1 armor gang. Roll with Spirit. On a 10+, nature responds without hesitation to fight for you. On a 7-9, the line between friend and foe becomes blurry, causing it to attack all around. On a miss, you have angered the spirits and nature deems you its enemy, at least for now.
If you have Status 3+ in the Night Circle, if you call on Nature's Vengeance, the spirits of the land respond instantly, attacking with greater precision and intelligence.

[MOVE] Manipulate the Elements: (requires Nature's Vengeance) The elements of the earth become as malleable as clay in the Werewolf's hands. When you call on the primal spirits of fire, water, earth, or air to perform a task for you, roll with Spirit. On a 10+, choose 2. On a 7-9, choose 1. On a miss, some catastrophe occurs as a result of your calling.
- The effect you desire comes to pass.
- You avoid paying nature's price.
- You retain control.


### Iron Masters

This Tribe follows the teachings of Red Wolf and embraces change and new ideas. They are closer to humanity, keeping up with technology and taking cities for their territory. Their tribal ban is "Honor your territory in all things." There are Werewolves of all Tribes in the cities, but the Iron Masters would argue that the others are never truly of the city — in the same geographical location, certainly, but never a part of it. The Iron Masters Tribe attracts those who have always sought new ways of doing things and are not afraid to take risks. Before the First Change, they tended to be quick adopters of new technology, idealistic social reformers, or anyone who rises above provincial and conventional thinking. They see how quickly and radically human society changes, and the idea of being left behind by that change is frustrating.

Moves for Iron Masters

[MOVE] Know Name: To understand something, one must first know its name. When you Figure Someone Out, ask an additional question that relates to the person's self or past.

[MOVE] Know the Path: The quickest and most efficient path is not always the safest, but so it goes. Name your Escape route and roll with Mind. On a 10+, you are gone. On a 7-9, you can go or stay, but if you go it costs you: leave something behind or take something with you — the MC will tell you what. On a miss, you are caught vulnerable, half-in and half-out.
If you have Status 3+ in the Mortalis Circle, when you Escape, you automatically find a safe haven in a nearby public space (a crowded cafe, a subway, or a parking garage) where no supernatural creature can immediately follow without breaking cover.

[MOVE] City Eyes: Windows are the eyes of a city; everything is recorded by the spirits in each pane of glass. For an urban hunter, this extra perspective can help track prey or be aware of danger. You place your hand to one window of a building to investigate through the windows of another building, looking either in or out with near-perfect clarity. When you do, roll with Mind. On a 10+, hold 2. On a 7-9, hold 1. Spend your hold 1 for 1 to ask the MC the following questions. On a miss, the MC makes a move as the window shatters under your hand.
- What happened here recently?
- Who is in control here?
- What should I be on the lookout for?

[MOVE] Maschinegeist: When you call on this gift, you merge your being with the spirit of a particular machine or electronic device. While accessing the machine, you are allowed to access its natural capabilities, but you must also see what the spirits (the MC) direct you to do. Roll with Spirit. On a 10+, mark Experience and take +1 forward if you do as instructed. On a 7-9, take +1 forward if you do as instructed, and destroy the machine in a fit of rage if you do not. On a miss, take -1 forward as you are lost in the Spirit world searching for answers.

[MOVE] Left Hand Spanner: (requires Maschinegeist) This gift temporarily disables a specific technological object — such as a cell phone, computer, or automobile engine — within arm's reach. The malfunction has no apparent external cause; in truth, you have interrupted the flow of spirit energy into the object, causing its breakdown. Roll with Spirit. On a 10+, choose 2. On a 7-9, choose 1 and mark corruption. On a miss, mark corruption and the overwhelming amount of energy leaves you drained (-1 forward).
- The energy is chaotic but you have control; you can single out which item you want to disable or turn off.
- Electronic items all around you start blinking in and out of power, making them useless (area).
- Too much power — electronic items explode, causing 1 harm to those holding or standing too close to them (area).


### Storm Lords

The Tribe of Winter Wolf seeks to lead all Werewolves through strength and noble example. Their tribal ban is "Allow no one to witness or to tend to your weakness." Werewolves who become Storm Lords tend not only to seek power, but to do something worthwhile with it. They brim with self-confidence, cavalierly taking what they want and working tirelessly to change the minds of those who do not respond favorably. This behavior becomes overbearing very quickly and pushes people away despite every good intention, but it is not inspired by simple greed or arrogance. They know that they must be strong and unyielding in order to live up to their responsibilities, never holding themselves back out of fear, weakness, or worry that their cousins might not approve of their methods. All that matters is doing what is required without fail and without complaint.

Moves for Storm Lords

[MOVE] Luna's Dictum: With this gift, you are able to articulate a command and compel obedience. Some Werewolves choose to speak these words in a soft, subtle whisper; others prefer bellowing orders. When you Persuade someone, roll with Spirit instead of Heart. On a 10+, you also take +1 forward on any attempt to persuade.

[MOVE] Voice of Command: At this level of dominance, the Werewolf learns to voice more complex and subtle decrees in a tone that demands attention and respect. A thread of anger runs through the Werewolf's words, making everyone in earshot take notice. When you give someone an order or a warning, roll with Heart. On a hit, choose 1:
- They do it, following your order or heeding your warning.
- They freeze.
- They back away, hands where you can see them.
- They attack you.
On a 10+, take +1 forward against them as well. On a miss, they do what they like and you take -1 forward against them.

[MOVE] Fog of War: A well-ordered battle plan seldom survives being put into action. This gift causes chaos to all who can hear the Werewolf sing, howl, or drum. Your gift grows from confidence after helping those around you. When you lend a hand to help the cause of an ally, gain 1 hold. Later, when you wish to disrupt the tactical patterns of others by isolating each individual from their allies or group, spend 1 hold. The allies of your targets gain no group benefits and cannot accept aid from others. This effect explicitly negates any group or gang bonuses. Spending hold to disrupt is automatic — no roll required.

[MOVE] Silent Fog: (requires Fog of War) With the objective of obscuring others, you call upon nature to bring a thick fog to the area around you that lasts for one scene or until dismissed. When you do, roll with Spirit. On a 10+, choose 4. On a 7-9, choose 3. This effect lasts for the scene.
- The fog is now so thick that one can only see and hear at close distances.
- Spend 1 additional to allow your own senses to work through the thickness of the enveloping fog.
- The spread of the fog increases, blanketing a city block.
- The fog reeks of something foul and numbing, causing everyone within to lose consciousness for a short while. PCs can Escape the fog with a success, but if you also choose to increase the blanketing size, the PC rolls at -2.
- Spend 1 additional to keep yourself from feeling the effects of the fog.
- The fog rattles with bits of electricity, blocking the sight of supernatural beings.
- Spend 1 additional to allow your own senses to seek those of supernatural beings.

[MOVE] Thunderbolt: (requires Spirit Walk) This gift implores a mighty thunderbird to turn its gaze earthward and fix its flashing eyes on a select target. Projectiles of pure lightning attack from above, inflicting a tremendous amount of damage to one target. When you command the heavens, roll with Spirit. On a 10+, choose 2. On a 7-9, choose 1 and mark corruption. On a miss, mark corruption and the MC makes a hard move as the spirits are displeased by your abuse of their power.
- Your enemy is stunned by the electricity (s-harm).
- 3 harm close, loud, and ap.
- Electricity arcs, causing 1 harm to all those within a short distance (area effect).
