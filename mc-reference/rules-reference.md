# City of Shadow — Rules Reference

**Source:** Urban Shadows 2e Core Book + WoD Supplement v2  
**Maintained:** `mc-reference/rules-reference.md`

The MC's authoritative quick-reference. When a rule is unclear, check here first.

> **WoD Supplement v2 mechanics** (Instinct Die, Extreme Failures, altered moves, WoD extensions) are in `mc-reference/wod-supplement.md`.

---

## 0. The Dice

Roll **2d6 + stat modifier.** One die is always the **Instinct Die** — track it with a different color.

| Total | Result |
|-------|--------|
| **10+** | Full hit |
| **7–9** | Partial hit — success with complication or cost |
| **6−** | Miss — MC makes a hard move; player marks XP |

**Cap:** No modifier may exceed +4 total.

**Extreme Failure:** If total is 6− AND the Instinct Die shows a 1, trigger an Extreme Failure (see `wod-supplement.md`).

---

## 1. Stats

Four stats. No others. Spirit is never renamed regardless of playbook.

| Stat | What It Covers |
|------|----------------|
| **Blood** | Violence, intimidation, physical endurance |
| **Heart** | Connection, persuasion, emotional reads |
| **Mind** | Research, planning, deception, noticing |
| **Spirit** | Supernatural resistance, willpower, rituals |

Values range −2 to +3 at character creation. No stat above +3 or below −2 to start.

## 2. Basic Moves

### Turn to Violence
**Trigger:** Player chooses force — attacking with intent to harm.
**Roll:** Blood

- **10+:** Inflict harm as established. Opponent chooses 1 from their list. Player also chooses 1.
- **7–9:** Inflict harm as established. Opponent chooses 1 from their list.
- **Miss:** Hard MC move.

**Opponent choices:** inflict harm on you | put you in a bad spot | create an opening to flee
**Player choices (10+ only):** inflict terrible harm (+1 harm) | take something from them | create an opportunity for an ally

### Escape a Situation
**Trigger:** Player takes advantage of an opening to flee. Must have an opening first.
**Roll:** Blood

- **10+:** Get away. Player chooses 1.
- **7–9:** Get away. Player AND MC each choose 1 (MC cannot choose same option).
- **Miss:** Hard MC move.

**Options:** suffer harm during escape | end up in another dangerous situation | leave something important behind | owe an NPC a Debt for their aid | give into base nature and mark corruption

### Persuade an NPC
**Trigger:** Convince an NPC using seduction, promises, or threats. Must have real leverage.
**Roll:** Heart — or invoke a Debt before rolling: add +3 (max +4 total)

- **10+:** They do what was asked (within scope of leverage).
- **7–9:** They counter or demand payment (Debt, favor, resources) before agreeing.
- **Miss:** They don't comply; hard MC move.

*If Debt invoked and player misses: Debt is not spent.*

### Figure Someone Out
**Trigger:** Study someone's body language, tone, or behavior to learn their motivations.
**Roll:** Mind

- **10+:** Ask 2 questions from the list.
- **7–9:** Ask 2 questions; they also ask 1 of the player.
- **Miss:** If in their Circle, still ask 1 question.

*In their Circle: ask one additional question even on a miss.*

**Questions:** Who's pulling your strings? | What's your beef with ___? | What are you hoping to get from ___? | What do you worry is going to happen? | How could I get you to ___? | How could I put you in my Debt?

### Mislead, Distract, or Trick
**Trigger:** Deceive someone to gain the upper hand. Must offer a plausible lie or trick.
**Roll:** Mind

- **10+:** Fooled. Player chooses 3 from list.
- **7–9:** Fooled. Player chooses 2 from list.
- **Miss:** Hard MC move.

**Options:** create an opportunity | expose a weakness or flaw | confuse them for some time | avoid further entanglement

### Keep Your Cool
**Trigger:** Pressure is on and no other move fits. Player declares what situation they're trying to avoid first.
**Roll:** Spirit

- **10+:** The declared situation doesn't come to pass.
- **7–9:** MC tells the player what it costs to get through.
- **Miss:** Hard MC move (not necessarily the declared threat).

### Let It Out
**Trigger:** Tap the darkness within to activate a supernatural ability from the playbook. Choose the specific ability before rolling.
**Roll:** Spirit

- **10+:** Mark corruption AND activate the ability. Ignore either the corruption OR the complications.
- **7–9:** Mark corruption AND activate the ability. MC says how it is costly, limited, or unstable.
- **Miss:** Hard MC move.

**Complication types:** Costly (full effect, real price) | Limited (partial effect — reduced scope) | Unstable (full effect but temporary or spiraling)

> **WoD note:** Channeling replaces this move for the Wizard playbook. See `wod-supplement.md`.

### Lend a Hand or Get in the Way
**Trigger:** After a PC has rolled, if another character can fictionally affect the action.
**Roll:** The rolling player's Circle.

- **10+:** Give them +1 or −2 to their roll.
- **7–9:** Give them +1 or −2, but you expose yourself to danger, entanglement, or cost.
- **Miss:** Hard MC move.

*Only one character can lend a hand and only one can get in the way on any given roll.*

### End of Session
At the end of every session:
1. Decide if the player grew closer to a Circle they engaged with — increase that Circle rating, decrease a different one. Mark one of the two Circles.
2. Note any Debts accrued or outstanding.

## 3. Circle Moves

*After any Circle move roll, mark that Circle for advancement — even on a miss.*

### Put a Face to a Name
**Trigger:** First time meeting someone new or hearing a new name.
**Roll:** Their Circle.

- **10+:** Know their reputation AND either learn something interesting/useful OR they owe you a Debt (player's choice).
- **7–9:** Know their reputation (MC tells what most people know).
- **Miss:** Don't know them, or you owe them — MC's choice.

### Hit the Streets
**Trigger:** Going out to seek a specific contact. Name who you're going to before rolling.
**Roll:** Their Circle.

- **10+:** They're available and have what you need.
- **7–9:** Choose 1: whoever you're going to is juggling their own problems | whatever you need is more costly than anticipated.
- **Miss:** Hard MC move.

### Study a Place of Power
**Trigger:** Investigating a sanctuary, gathering spot, or place of power beyond a quick glance.
**Roll:** The Circle that controls it.

- **10+:** MC reveals something not what it seems AND player asks one relevant question about the Circle's politics — take +1 forward acting on the answer.
- **7–9:** MC reveals something not what it seems.
- **Miss:** Hard MC move.

## 4. Debt Moves

### Do Someone a Favor
**Trigger:** Automatic — any time you meaningfully help someone without receiving recompense.
**Effect:** They owe you a Debt. Must be real and meaningful — not incidental.

### Cash In a Debt
**Trigger:** Remind the debtor why they owe you and make the request.
**Effect:** If honored, Debt is erased.

**On a PC, demand one of:**
- Answer a question honestly
- Do you a favor at moderate cost
- Lend a hand to your efforts
- Get in the way of someone else
- Erase a Debt they hold on someone
- Give you a Debt they hold on someone else

**On an NPC, demand one of:**
- Answer a question honestly
- Arrange a meeting with an NPC in their Circle
- Grant a worthy boon or useful gift
- Erase a Debt they hold on someone
- Give you a Debt they hold on someone else

*Invoking a Debt also adds +3 to a Persuade an NPC roll (max +4 total). If the roll misses, the Debt is not spent.*

### Refuse to Honor a Debt
**Roll:** The difference in Status between you and your creditor.

- **10+:** Weasel out for now. Debt still stands.
- **7–9:** Weasel out, but owe them an additional Debt OR mark corruption.
- **Miss:** Either honor the Debt in full OR erase all Debts that Circle owes you and take −1 ongoing to Status with their Circle until time passes.

## 5. City Moves

*PCs may make city moves equal to their highest Status each faction turn.*

### Status-1 Moves

**Weaken Someone's Standing** — Spread destructive rumors about a target, roll with Status in their Circle.
- **10+:** −1 ongoing to their moves during the faction turn. An enemy brings you damaging true information about them.
- **7–9:** −1 ongoing to their moves during the faction turn.
- **Miss:** Target traces it to you and catches you vulnerable.

**Put Out the Word** — Ask around your Circle for something specific, roll with Status.
- **10+:** It shows up in an ally's hands; yours for a Debt.
- **7–9:** A rival has it — they want a favor or gift in addition to a Debt.
- **Miss:** Terrible strings attached — you've made an enemy of a Status-3 NPC from another Circle.

**Consult Your Contacts** — Ask around a specific Circle for information. Name the Circle, roll with Status in that Circle.
- **10+:** Ask 3 questions; hold 2.
- **7–9:** Ask 3 questions; hold 1.
- **Miss:** Ask 1 question; hold 1; one contact asks a hard question about your loyalties — answer honestly, mark corruption, or owe them a Debt.

*Spend hold (1 for 1) before time passes to take a 10+ instead of rolling when the information aids a move.*

**Questions:** What conflict is everyone talking about? | What happened to ___? | What has ___ been up to recently? | Who is responsible for ___? | Who has a Debt on ___?

**Tend to Your Business** — Lie low and mind your affairs during the faction turn, roll with no modifier.
- **10+:** You tend to your affairs. An old friend or new ally approaches — mark corruption if you turn them down. Ask the MC one question.
- **7–9:** Same, but mark corruption to refuse.
- **Miss:** An NPC interrupts with a Debt to cash in, dragging you into a Circle mess.

### Status-2 Moves

**Marshal Forces** — Call your Circle to undermine a faction, roll with Status.
- **10+:** A powerful NPC answers — give them a Debt to reduce a target faction's Strength or weaken their asset hold.
- **7–9:** Allies are slow — give them another Debt OR publicly push them into the conflict.
- **Miss:** No support; enemies get an opportunity to strike.

**Lay Claim** — Publicly claim an asset you've seized, roll with Status.
- **10+:** Claim recognized. Choose all 3.
- **7–9:** Claim recognized. Choose 1.
- **Miss:** A Status-3 NPC also desires the asset and targets one of your allies or assets.

*Options:* your claim chokes off factional retaliation | your claim unites allies behind you | your claim doesn't cost a Debt

**Recruit New Allies** — Reach out to another Circle for help. Declare the Circle, roll with Status in that Circle.
- **10+:** Someone interested is available. Offer a Debt; on call until after the next faction turn. Choose 3 qualities.
- **7–9:** Someone available. Offer a Debt. Choose 2 qualities.
- **Miss:** Someone in your own Circle spreads word of your weakness — −1 ongoing to Status until you prove strength.

*Qualities:* unfailingly honest | exceptionally skilled | notably discreet | aggressively focused

### Status-3 Moves
At Status-3 (only reached by seizing or establishing a faction), PCs gain access to full faction mechanics. See US2 pp.255–268.

## 6. Harm & Healing

**Harm track:** 4 boxes. Mark when taking harm.

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Faint (1 box) | Bruises, scrapes |
| Serious (2 boxes) | Cuts, injuries |
| Critical (3 boxes) | Serious wounds |
| 4 boxes marked | Near death — Death's Door |

Clear harm through rest, care, or specific moves.

**Scars** — Permanent consequences from catastrophic harm:
- Shattered: −1 Blood
- Crushed: −1 Heart
- Fractured: −1 Mind
- Broken: −1 Spirit

## 7. Corruption

**Track:** 5 boxes. Mark when:
- Using Let It Out on a 7–9
- Specific move triggers require it
- Certain Extreme Failures
- Choosing it as an escape cost

**Corruption advances:** When track is full, choose a corruption advance (dark powers at a cost) and clear the track. See US2 p.192 for full list.

## 8. Circles, Status & Rating

**The four Circles:** Mortalis (humans who know) | Night (vampires, ghosts) | Power (mages, witches) | Wild (shifters, fae, spirits)

**Circle Ratings** — the roll modifier when acting in that Circle's domain. Can be negative. Tracked in `state.json` as `circle_ratings`.

**Circle Status** — social standing within that faction. Affects story access, NPC reactions, and move triggers. Tracked in `state.json` as `circle_status`.

These are always tracked separately. Never conflate them.

| Status Level | What It Means |
|-------------|---------------|
| Status-0 | Outsider; known but not trusted |
| Status-1 | Established; Debts are honored |
| Status-2 | Power player; can command Circle resources |
| Status-3 | Faction leader; full faction mechanics |

---

## 9. Advancement

**XP track:** 7 slots. Mark XP through Circle moves, end of session, and specific move rewards. When filled: take a standard advance and clear the track.

**Standard advances (before 5 total advances taken):**
- Take a new move from your playbook
- Take a move from another playbook
- Take +1 to a stat (max +3)
- Take +1 Status in any Circle (max +3)
- Take +1 Circle Rating in any Circle (max +3)
- Retire your character to safety

**After 5 advances:**
- Gain a new playbook move (from any playbook)
- Change your Circle
- Obtain Status −2 in a Circle (become notorious)

## 12. MC Moves

*Never announce moves by name. Describe the fiction only.*

**Hard moves (on a miss):**
Separate them | Capture someone | Put someone in a spot | Trade harm for harm | Tell them the requirements | Turn their move back on them | Offer an opportunity with cost | Put them on the clock

**Soft moves (on 7–9 without specific options, or to signal trouble):**
Foreshadow danger | Show trouble ahead | Offer opportunity with cost | Take something away temporarily | Reveal an unwelcome truth

---

## 13. Faction Turn

Conducted when time passes. Each faction pursues its agenda.

Factions have: Purpose, Structure, Mood, Size, Strength.

**Faction moves:** Claim territory | Bolster strength | Attack a weakness | Pursue agenda | Make an offer

PCs with Status-3 participate directly in the faction turn. See US2 pp.255–268 for full rules.
